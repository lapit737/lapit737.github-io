About Me

Hi there!

I’m Tamar and I am a journalist, writer and poet. I’ve been a writer for as long as I can remember, at least according to my fourth grade journals, which are filled with made up fairytales and poems about my sister and friends. I discovered my passion for journalism freshman year of college at The New School when I took my first journalism class. I was cripplingly shy at the time and didn’t say much except to voice my admiration for Joan Didion and David Foster Wallace’s essay “Roger Federer as Religious Experience.” But journalism forced me to speak. To strangers at first- one of our assignments was to accost people in the street to ask them what they thought of President Obama’s first term. After more than one “I don’t have time,” and “no thanks,” I finally landed on a couple of people who would answer and who became the sources for my first story. Journalism pushed me to get out there, to talk to people, discover what makes them angry, happy and what they ultimately want- from their society, their politicians, their peers and themselves. I consider journalism a valued tradition and art form, combining research, clarity, and information dissemination.I am the Editor in Chief for my school newspaper, The New School Free Press. I was fortunate enough to receive scholarships to attend the 2015 Associate Collegiate Press Conference in Los Angeles, the 2015 Computer Assisted Reporting (CAR) conference in Atlanta and South by Southwest as well as to write about my experiences. As part of The New School’s Journalism & Design program I’ve been working on data-driven stories and a more design and digital geared way of thinking about stories- from pitch to publication.
